# Content Equivalence Audit

This draft follows a same-content, different-explicitness design.

For each case:

- `raw` contains the substantive information in natural prose;
- `annotated` adds relation tags without adding substantive facts;
- `compiled` restructures the same information into typed fields.

Known risk: because these are adapted/paraphrased cases, independent review is recommended to verify that the annotated or compiled condition does not add a relation that was not reasonably inferable from the raw condition.
